Hakgala Botanical Gardens

Our Birds

Best Bird Watching Sites in Sri Lanka, covered in our tour plans

Hakgala Botanic Gardens, at 1,745m above sea level, span 28 hectares beneath the towering 2,200m Hakgala Rock ("Elephant’s Jaw Rock"). The terraced gardens overlook the Uva Valley, offering stunning views of the Madulsima and Namunukula mountain ranges.

Sri Lanka white Eye

The **Sri Lanka White-eye** (*Zosterops ceylonensis*) is a small, greenish-yellow bird endemic to Sri Lanka's highlands. It has a distinctive white eye-ring, bright yellow throat, and olive-green upperparts. Found in montane forests and gardens, it moves in flocks, feeding on insects, nectar, and fruits, often accompanied by cheerful, high-pitched calls.

Sri Lanka Bush Wabler

The **Sri Lanka Bush Warbler** (*Elaphrornis palliseri*) is a shy, brownish bird endemic to Sri Lanka's highlands. It inhabits dense undergrowth in montane forests, often skulking near the ground. It has a plain olive-brown back, rufous flanks, and a paler throat. Known for its sharp, repetitive call, it is more often heard than seen.

Sri Lanka Wood Pigeon

The **Sri Lanka Wood Pigeon** (*Columba torringtoniae*) is a large, endemic pigeon found in Sri Lanka's highland forests. It has a grayish-purple body, a checkerboard-patterned neck patch, and a deep cooing call. Typically seen in pairs or small groups, it feeds on fruits and berries in the forest canopy.

Dull Blue Flycatcher

The **Dull-blue Flycatcher** (*Eumyias sordidus*) is a small, endemic bird of Sri Lanka's montane forests. It has a largely blue plumage with an intense blue forehead, a black face, and a pale gray belly. Found in the understory, it is often seen alone or in pairs, emitting melodic warbling notes and short "chip" calls.

Indian Blue Robin

The **Indian Blue Robin** (*Larvivora brunnea*) is a small, striking bird found in dense undergrowth of forests and plantations, particularly in Sri Lanka's hills during winter. Males have vivid blue upperparts, a white throat stripe, and an orange chest, while females are duller with brownish tones. It is shy and often forages on the ground, flipping leaves for insects.

Canary Flycatcher

The **Canary Flycatcher** (*Culicicapa ceylonensis*) is a small, active bird with a bright yellow belly, olive-gray upperparts, and a short crest. Found in forests and wooded areas, it flits energetically while catching insects and is known for its sweet, high-pitched calls.

Black Bird

The **Blackbird** (*Turdus merula*) is a medium-sized songbird with glossy black plumage in males and a bright yellow bill, while females are brown with streaked underparts. Found in forests, gardens, and urban areas, it is known for its melodious, fluty song, especially at dawn and dusk. It forages on the ground for insects, worms, and berries.

Yellow earned Bulbul

The **Yellow-eared Bulbul** (*Pycnonotus penicillatus*) is a small, vibrant bird found in the forests of Sri Lanka and southern India. It has distinctive yellow ear patches, a dark head, and olive-green upperparts. Its underparts are pale, and it is known for its melodic calls. This species is often seen in the mid-canopy, foraging for fruits and insects.

×
Pied Thrush

The **Pied Thrush** (*Turdus nigriceps*) is a striking bird with a black head, back, and wings, contrasting with its white underparts and bright orange-brown sides. Found in Sri Lanka’s forests, it is often seen foraging on the ground for insects and fruit. Known for its melodic song, it is a relatively solitary bird, typically found in dense, moist woodlands.

Canary Fly catcher

The **Canary Flycatcher** (*Culicicapa ceylonensis*) is a small, vibrant bird found in Sri Lanka's forests. It has a striking yellow belly, olive-gray upperparts, and a short crest. This active bird is often seen flitting around catching insects. Its cheerful call adds to its appeal in the lush forest environment.

×
Indian Pitta

The **Indian Pitta** (*Pitta brachyura*) is a brightly colored bird with a distinctive appearance, featuring green upperparts, a buffy crown, black eye-stripe, and a vibrant red vent. It is commonly found in forests and dense undergrowth, where it forages for insects. Known for its lively, two-note whistle call, the Indian Pitta is active during the early morning and dusk hours.

×
Green Sandpiper

The **Green Sandpiper** (*Tringa ochropus*) is a small wader with a distinctive greenish-brown plumage, often appearing in wetland habitats. It has a white underside with dark spots and a long, slender bill. Known for its graceful, steady foraging along shorelines and muddy banks, it feeds on small invertebrates. It also produces a soft, melodious call.
