Our Birds

Best Bird Watching Sites in Sri Lanka, covered in our tour plans

Located near the iconic Sigiriya Rock and adjacent to Minneriya National Park, this sanctuary features dry evergreen, shrub, and deciduous forests. It's perfect for photographing forest birds like Peacocks, Eagles, and Indian Pittas. The area is also home to migrating elephants, Sambar Deer, and two monkey species.

Peacocks

The Indian Peafowl (*Pavo cristatus*) in Sri Lanka is a vibrant bird with males showcasing iridescent blue-green plumage and ornate tail feathers. Females are brownish. Found in dry lowlands, they forage for grains, insects, and reptiles, with loud calls heard during breeding season.

×
Eagles

Sri Lanka is home to several eagle species, including the Crested Serpent Eagle, Changeable Hawk-Eagle, and White-bellied Sea Eagle. These raptors inhabit forests, wetlands, and coastal areas, hunting small mammals, birds, and fish. They are known for their keen eyesight, strong talons, and distinctive calls.

×
Migrant Indian Pitta

The Indian Pitta is a colorful migrant bird in Sri Lanka, known for its vibrant plumage with shades of green, blue, and red. Found in forests and gardens, it is a striking sight during its seasonal visit. It feeds on insects and small invertebrates, often seen hopping on the ground.
