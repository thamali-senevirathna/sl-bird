Our Birds

Best Bird Watching Sites in Sri Lanka, covered in our tour plans

Udawatte Kelle Sanctuary, Dunumadalawa Forest reserve and Royal Botanical Gardens, Peradeniya are good places for birds including a few endemics such as Layard's Parakeets, Sri Lanka Hanging Parrot, Yellow fronted Barbet, Brown capped Babbler, White rumped Shama, Tickell's Blue Flycatcher etc.

Layard's Parakeets

Layard’s Parakeet is a bright green, long-tailed parrot with a gray hood and black throat. Males have a red-orange beak, while females have a dark beak. Found in small groups in humid forests and wooded areas of Sri Lanka’s Wet Zone and hills, its distinctive gray hood and black throat set it apart from other long-tailed parrots. Known for its noisy, raucous calls.

×
Sri Lanka Hanging Parrot

The tiny, short-tailed bright green parrot, native to Sri Lanka, has a red bill and rump, with males featuring a scarlet crown. Found in small groups in the Wet Zone forests, it is easily identified by its small size and short tail. Its flight call is a metallic “zit-zit-zit,” and it also makes a drawn-out “zeeet.”

×
Yellow fronted Barbet

The green barbet with a blue face, yellow forecrown, and speckled underparts is found in the canopy of forests in Sri Lanka's lowlands and foothills. Common in the Wet Zone, it is distinguished by its yellow forehead and moustache, blue face, and dappled underparts. Its song is a repetitive “ke-cheer,” with calls including a rolling “pup-pup-pup-pup.”

×
Brown capped Babbler

The small brown babbler with cinnamon underparts and a blackish brown cap is found on the ground in forests, edges, and scrub areas of Sri Lanka. Typically seen in pairs, it is distinguished by its bold cap and underpart colors. Its song is a three-note whistle “wit-wi-yoo” with other complex, downslurred whistles.

×
White rumped Shama

This thrushlike bird has a long tail, with males being glossy black above and chestnut below, while females are paler. Juveniles are reddish brown with rusty wingbars. Found in dense forests and plantations, it is often poached for the cagebird trade, causing population declines. Its song is a varied series of whistles and mimicked bird calls, with a sharp “tsick” call. It has been introduced to Hawaii and Taiwan.

×
Tickell's Blue Flycatcher

Tickell's Blue Flycatcher is a small, striking bird in Sri Lanka, with vibrant blue upperparts, a white belly, and orange on the throat and breast. It is typically found in forests and wooded areas, where it perches to catch insects in flight. Known for its active, inquisitive nature, it has a melodious song and a series of sharp calls.
