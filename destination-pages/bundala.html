Bundala National Park

Our Birds

Best Bird Watching Sites in Sri Lanka, covered in our tour plans

This is the First Ramsor site in Sri Lanka and it is the most important wetland for birds outside the Northern Province . The lagoons and saltants of the park are among the most important wintering areas for migratory shore birds in the Country.

Greater Flamingo

A tall, elegant bird with pinkish-white plumage and a curved bill, found in Sri Lanka's lagoons and salt pans during migration.

Sri Lanka Jungle Fowl

The national bird of Sri Lanka, this colorful forest bird has bright red, orange, and yellow plumage, resembling a domestic rooster.

Great Egret

A large white wader with a long neck and yellow bill, seen in wetlands and paddy fields, hunting fish and amphibians.

×
Asian Openbill

A medium-sized stork with a distinct gap in its bill, commonly found in paddy fields feeding on snails and small aquatic animals.

×
Black-winged Stilt

A striking wader with long pink legs and black-and-white plumage, frequenting shallow wetlands and lagoons.

×
Yellow-wattled Lapwing

A ground-dwelling bird with a brown body, yellow wattles near its face, and a preference for open dry habitats.

×
Painted Stork

A large, colorful stork with a pink-tipped white body, black markings, and a long yellow bill, found in wetlands.

×
Plovers

Migratory shorebirds with compact bodies and short bills, seen in large numbers on Sri Lanka's coastal mudflats and wetlands.

×
Sandpipers

Small waders with slender bills and long legs, found foraging in shallow waters along Sri Lanka's coasts during migration.

×
Terns

Graceful seabirds with slender bodies, forked tails, and sharp beaks, often seen diving for fish near Sri Lanka's shores.

×
Ducks

Waterfowl with broad bills and webbed feet, commonly found in Sri Lanka's lakes and wetlands, including both resident and migratory species.

Rosy Starling

A striking migratory bird with pinkish plumage and a black head, seen in flocks during winter in Sri Lanka's dry zones.

Brahminy Myna

A small bird with pale orange-brown plumage, a black crown, and a yellow bill, often found in gardens and open woodlands in Sri Lanka.
