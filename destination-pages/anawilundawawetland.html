Anawilundawa Wetland

Our Birds

Best Bird Watching Sites in Sri Lanka, covered in our tour plans

Located near the iconic Sigiriya Rock and adjacent to Minneriya National Park, this sanctuary features dry evergreen, shrub, and deciduous forests. It's perfect for photographing forest birds like Peacocks, Eagles, and Indian Pittas. The area is also home to migrating elephants, Sambar Deer, and two monkey species.

Pied Kingfisher

A striking black-and-white kingfisher found near lakes, rivers, and lagoons. It hovers over water before diving to catch fish, making it highly distinctive in behavior and appearance.

×
Common Kingfisher

A small, brightly colored bird with a blue back, orange underparts, and a sharp bill. It perches quietly by streams or ponds, diving swiftly for fish.

×
Stork-billed Kingfisher

The largest kingfisher in Sri Lanka, with a stout red bill, greenish-blue upperparts, and buff underparts. Found near rivers and wetlands, it feeds on fish and frogs.

×
Black Bittern

A secretive, dark-colored heron with a yellowish streak on the neck. Prefers dense reeds and marshes, often remaining hidden and motionless.

×
Yellow Bittern

A small, pale yellow heron with a slender body, seen in reed beds and paddy fields. It is known for its stealthy movements while foraging for small prey.

×
Cinnamon Bittern

A chestnut-colored heron with a slender build, found in wetlands and paddy fields. It remains elusive, blending well with surrounding vegetation.

×
Crimson-fronted Barbet

A small, vibrant green bird with a crimson forehead and yellow face. Common in wooded areas, it feeds on fruits and is known for its loud, repetitive calls.

×
Black-rumped Flameback

A striking woodpecker with golden-yellow wings, black back, and red crest. Found in forests and gardens, it feeds on insects by hammering tree trunks.

×
Blue-faced Malkoha

The Blue-faced Malkoha (Phaenicophaeus viridirostris) is a striking bird with a vivid blue face patch, a long tail, and olive-green plumage. Found in Sri Lanka's forests and scrublands, it forages for insects and small reptiles in the canopy.

×
Brown Fish Owl

The Brown Fish Owl (Ketupa zeylonensis) is a large nocturnal bird with bright yellow eyes, ear tufts, and brown streaked plumage. Common near waterbodies in Sri Lanka, it preys on fish, amphibians, and small mammals.
