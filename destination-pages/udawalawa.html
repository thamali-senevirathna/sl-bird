Udawalawa National Park

Our Birds

Best Bird Watching Sites in Sri Lanka, covered in our tour plans

Located near the iconic Sigiriya Rock and adjacent to Minneriya National Park, this sanctuary features dry evergreen, shrub, and deciduous forests. It's perfect for photographing forest birds like Peacocks, Eagles, and Indian Pittas. The area is also home to migrating elephants, Sambar Deer, and two monkey species.

Sri Lanka Junglefowl

The national bird of Sri Lanka, this brightly colored forest bird features a fiery red comb with a yellow center and vibrant plumage. Found in forests and scrublands, it forages on the ground for seeds and insects.

×
Sri Lanka Grey Hornbill

An endemic bird with a gray body, curved bill, and white-tipped tail. Found in forests and gardens, it feeds on fruits, insects, and small animals.

×
Malabar Pied Hornbill

A large black-and-white bird with a prominent casque on its bill. Found in lowland forests, it feeds primarily on fruits and occasionally on small animals.

×
Woolly-necked Stork

A tall wader with a white woolly neck, dark body, and long legs. Common in wetlands, it feeds on fish, frogs, and small reptiles.

×
Black-headed Ibis

A large wader with a white body, bare black head, and curved bill. Found in wetlands, it feeds on invertebrates, fish, and carrion.

×
Black-shouldered Kite

A small raptor with striking red eyes, white body, and black shoulders. Found in grasslands and open areas, it hunts small mammals and birds.

×
Crested Serpent Eagle

A medium-sized eagle with a bold crest, yellow eyes, and dark plumage. Found in forests, it preys on snakes and other reptiles.

×
Changeable Hawk-Eagle

A versatile predator with a crest and variable plumage, ranging from pale to dark. Found in forests and open areas, it hunts birds and small mammals.

×
Shikra

A small, agile hawk with a gray body, barred chest, and sharp yellow eyes. Found in open woodlands, it preys on birds, lizards, and insects.

×
Sirkeer Malkoha

A large, shy bird with a long tail, reddish-brown plumage, and curved bill. Found in dry scrublands, it feeds on insects and small animals.

×
Blue-faced Malkoha

A striking bird with blue skin around the eyes, greenish upperparts, and a long tail. Found in forests, it forages for insects and small vertebrates in trees.
